Ugali Recipes

Ugali is a staple food in many African countries, made from maize flour (cornmeal) and water. It is a simple yet versatile dish that can be paired with various sides such as vegetables, meats, and sauces. Here are a few popular Ugali recipes:

Basic Ugali Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups maize flour (cornmeal)
  • 4 cups water
  • Pinch of salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a pot.
  2. Add a pinch of salt if desired.
  3. Gradually add the maize flour to the boiling water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Reduce the heat and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  5. Once thickened, cover the pot and let it cook for a few more minutes on low heat.
  6. Remove from heat and let it sit for a couple of minutes before serving.
  7. Serve hot with your favorite side dishes.

Ugali with Sukuma Wiki (Collard Greens)

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of sukuma wiki (collard greens), chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  4. Add the chopped sukuma wiki and stir well.
  5. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the greens are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the sukuma wiki alongside hot ugali.

Ugali with Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef, cut into cubes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: carrots, potatoes, or other vegetables

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the beef cubes and brown them on all sides.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add beef broth or water, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 1-2 hours until the beef is tender. Add vegetables if using, and cook until they are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the beef stew hot with ugali.

Enjoy your Ugali meals!
